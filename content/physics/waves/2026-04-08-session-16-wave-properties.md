## The Hook: Your Headphones at 7am

Picture this: it's 7am, you're making coffee, phone connected to your headphones. You crank the volume up. The music gets louder — but the pitch doesn't change. The bass stays bass, the treble stays treble. Just... more.

## Remember Where We Left Off

Last session: a wave is a **travelling disturbance** — the medium oscillates, but the pattern propagates. Transverse waves oscillate perpendicular to their direction of travel (rope, light, pond ripples). Longitudinal waves oscillate parallel (sound, Slinky — compressions and rarefactions).

## Concept 1: Amplitude — The Height of the Wave, Not Its Speed

The most common confusion in wave physics: people assume a bigger, louder, brighter wave must be a *faster* wave.

It isn't.

**Amplitude (A)** is the maximum displacement of the medium from its equilibrium (resting) position. On a diagram of a transverse wave, it's the height from the centre line to the crest — or the depth to the trough. Always measured from equilibrium to peak, not crest to trough (crest-to-trough is 2A, the full excursion).

**What amplitude actually represents: energy.**

- **Crank the headphones:** same frequency (same pitch, same wavelength), higher amplitude — more energy pushing your eardrums per second → louder
- **Gentle ripple vs a tsunami:** similar wavelengths, vastly different amplitude, vastly different energy
- **Bright vs dim light of the same colour:** same frequency (same colour), different amplitude (more photons per second, same energy each → brighter)

**Key rule:** Amplitude does **not** determine how fast the wave travels. Speed is set by the medium (air, water, steel), not the source.

Symbol: **A**, measured in metres (m) for mechanical displacement waves.

## Concept 2: Frequency and Period — The Tempo of the Wave

**Frequency (f)** is the number of complete wave cycles that pass a fixed point per second.

Unit: **Hertz (Hz)** — 1 Hz = 1 complete cycle per second.

Real numbers to build intuition:

| Note / Source | Frequency | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Middle C (piano) | 262 Hz | 262 complete pressure cycles reach your ear every second |
| One octave up | 524 Hz | Exactly double |
| Bass guitar lowest (E1) | 41 Hz | Slow, long cycles — you feel them in your sternum |
| Top of piano | 4,186 Hz | Very rapid, short cycles |
| Human hearing range | 20 Hz – 20,000 Hz | The full band your ears can detect |
| Wi-Fi (2.4 GHz) | 2,400,000,000 Hz | 2.4 billion cycles per second |

**Period (T)** is the time for one complete cycle. It's the inverse of frequency:

> **T = 1/f** (and f = 1/T)

Middle C at 262 Hz has a period of:
T = 1/262 ≈ **0.0038 seconds** (3.8 milliseconds per cycle)

**Key rule:** Frequency is a property of the *source* — the thing generating the wave. A piano string vibrates at 262 Hz because of its tension and length. That frequency is fixed by the source, not by the medium it travels through.

---

## Concept 3: Wavelength — The Spatial Fingerprint

Frequency tells you *how often* a cycle occurs in time. **Wavelength (λ, lambda)** tells you *how long* one complete cycle is in space.

More precisely: wavelength is the distance between two consecutive identical points on the wave — crest to crest, trough to trough, compression to compression. It's the physical length of one full oscillation measured in the direction of travel.

Units: **metres (m)** — or sub-units (nanometres, centimetres) for very short wavelengths.

**The core relationship:**
- High frequency = **short wavelength** (rapid cycling → crests appear close together in space)
- Low frequency = **long wavelength** (slow cycling → crests spread far apart)

Real wavelengths to build intuition:

| Source | Frequency | Wavelength in air |
|---|---|---|
| Bass guitar (A2) | 110 Hz | ~3.1 m (longer than your dining table) |
| Middle C | 262 Hz | ~1.3 m (about your height) |
| Top of piano | 4,186 Hz | ~8 cm (width of your hand) |
| Wi-Fi (2.4 GHz) | 2.4 × 10⁹ Hz | ~12.5 cm |
| Visible light (orange) | ~500 THz | ~600 nm (0.0000006 m) |

## Connecting the Four Properties

| Property | Symbol | Unit | What it measures | Set by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Amplitude | A | m | Energy of the wave | Source |
| Frequency | f | Hz | Pace of oscillation | Source |
| Period | T | s | Duration of one cycle | Source (T = 1/f) |
| Wavelength | λ | m | Length of one cycle in space | Source AND medium |
| Speed | v | m/s | Rate of propagation | Medium |

**The key insight (Session 17 preview):** Wave speed is set by the medium, not the source. In the same medium, v is fixed. So if f increases (higher pitch), λ *must* decrease.

**Double the frequency → half the wavelength. Speed unchanged.**

---

## The Maths

**Period and Frequency:**
$$T = \frac{1}{f} \qquad f = \frac{1}{T}$$

**Wave Equation (full derivation next session):**
$$v = f \times \lambda$$

**Amplitude and Energy:**
$$E \propto A^2$$

## Real-World Grounding

**Wi-Fi at home:** λ = v/f = 3×10⁸ / 2.4×10⁹ ≈ 12.5 cm. This is why Wi-Fi passes through walls (λ comparable to gap sizes) but 5 GHz Wi-Fi (λ ≈ 6 cm) is absorbed more readily.

**Medical ultrasound:** 1–20 MHz. λ in soft tissue ≈ 0.1–1.5 mm. Shorter λ = finer resolution, but more absorption (less depth penetration). Frequency is a design trade-off.

**Colour of light:** Colour = frequency. Brightness = amplitude. You cannot change the colour of light by making it brighter.
