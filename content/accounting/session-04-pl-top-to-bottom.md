# Accounting — Session 4: The P&L Top to Bottom

> *From first pound of revenue to final loss — every line matters*

---

## What You Already Know

In Session 3, you learned that gross margin is the gap between what you earn and what it costs to deliver — for Memrise, that means revenue minus App Store fees and payment processing. You know that Memrise runs at roughly 73-74% gross margin, which is healthy for a subscription software business. Today you'll see how that gross margin sits inside the full P&L and what happens to it by the time you reach the bottom line.

## The simplest true statement

A P&L is a story: it starts with all the money that came in, and then shows you, one line at a time, where it all went.

---

## The Full Stack: Memrise January 2026

Here is the approximate P&L structure for Memrise in January 2026. These are teaching numbers — realistic, but rounded and simplified for clarity.

| Line | Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | ~£443k | Subscriptions (B2C + B2B) |
| Cost of Revenue (COGS) | ~£117k | App Store fees, payment processing |
| **Gross Profit** | ~£326k | 73.5% gross margin |
| Payroll | ~£550k | Salaries, NI, pension — the dominant cost |
| Running Costs | ~£76k | AWS, Cloudflare, tooling, office |
| Sales & Marketing | ~£150k | Paid acquisition, B2B sales cost |
| R&D | ~£100k+ | Engineering, product, design (OpEx portion) |
| **EBITDA** | ~-£575k | Gross profit minus all operating expenses |
| Depreciation & Amortisation | ~£30k | Non-cash — spread cost of prior investments |
| Interest & Tax | ~£15k | Debt interest, minor tax items |
| **Net Loss** | ~-£620k | The bottom line |

Read this as a waterfall. Each line is a layer being subtracted. By the time you've subtracted all operating expenses from gross profit, you reach EBITDA. From there, depreciation, amortisation, interest, and tax take you to net income (or, in this case, net loss).

---

## Why the Waterfall Shape Matters

The layered structure isn't just cosmetic — it tells you *where* the money problem lives.

Memrise's gross margin of 73.5% is genuinely strong. If you stopped the P&L at gross profit, you'd think the business was in decent shape. The problem is the operating cost structure: payroll alone (~£550k) is 1.7x the entire revenue line. That's not unusual for a growth-stage software company, but it makes the path to profitability very clear: either revenue grows substantially, or the cost base contracts.

This is why investors and CFOs read P&Ls top to bottom, not just at the bottom line. Each layer reveals a different kind of lever:

- **Gross margin** → pricing, channel mix, supplier terms
- **Payroll** → headcount decisions, seniority mix, location
- **Running costs** → infrastructure efficiency, contract renegotiation
- **S&M** → customer acquisition efficiency, channel ROI
- **R&D** → build vs. buy, capitalisation decisions (more on this in Session 8)

---

## EBITDA vs Net Loss: the £45k Gap

You'll notice that EBITDA (~-£575k) and net loss (~-£620k) differ by about £45k. That gap is depreciation, amortisation, interest, and tax. These items sit *below* EBITDA in the P&L.

For now, the key insight is that EBITDA tries to capture the operational performance of the business — stripping out financing decisions (interest) and accounting conventions (depreciation). Net loss includes everything. Neither is more "true" than the other; they answer different questions. Session 5 goes deep on this.

---

## Reading a P&L as a Decision-Maker

When you look at Memrise's P&L, you're not just reading history — you're scanning for levers. A few questions worth asking every month:

1. **Did revenue grow vs. last month?** If not, why not?
2. **Did gross margin hold?** A margin decline signals a problem in delivery costs or channel mix.
3. **Is payroll growing faster than revenue?** That's a warning sign.
4. **Is the EBITDA loss narrowing or widening?** The trend matters more than the absolute number.

The P&L is a management instrument, not a compliance document. Companies that treat it as the latter tend to be surprised by the former.

---

## Three Concepts to Remember

1. **Revenue minus COGS equals Gross Profit** — this is the first and most important subtraction. Everything below it is about how efficiently you run the business.
2. **Payroll dominates the OpEx stack** — at Memrise, it's larger than revenue itself. Every headcount decision is a P&L decision.
3. **EBITDA and net loss answer different questions** — EBITDA is operational; net loss is total. The gap between them is D&A, interest, and tax.
